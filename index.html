Understanding the modern supply chain software landscape

Supply chain software includes a wide range of platforms designed to support planning, visibility, decision-making, and execution across complex operations.

Explore categories View alternatives
Overview

How supply chain platforms are commonly grouped

Enterprise supply chain technology is often discussed through overlapping terms such as planning, control towers, visibility, orchestration, and execution. Grouping platforms by primary function makes the market easier to evaluate.

A practical evaluation lens

Some platforms are built to store and structure operational data. Others are designed to support planning, provide insight, or help teams coordinate action across the network when conditions change.

Platform categories

Four useful categories for comparing platforms

These categories help distinguish between systems built to record transactions, create plans, generate insight, and support coordinated response.

Category 1

Systems of Record

Transaction-focused systems that capture operational history and support core business processes.

Category 2

Systems of Planning

Platforms designed to support forecasts, scenarios, and structured planning cycles.

Category 3

Systems of Insight

Platforms that help teams unify data, interpret signals, and understand what is happening across the network.

Category 4

Systems of Action

Platforms that help teams coordinate decisions and execution when supply chain conditions change.

Evaluation criteria

What buyers typically compare

Evaluation often comes down to how quickly a platform can deliver value, how complex it is to maintain, and whether it supports planning, insight, execution, or some combination of the three.

Criteria What it helps evaluate
Deployment speed How quickly the platform can deliver operational value
Modeling complexity How much engineering effort is required to configure and maintain the system
Execution capability Whether the platform helps teams act, not just analyze
Cross-functional fit How well the platform connects planning, procurement, logistics, inventory, and operations
