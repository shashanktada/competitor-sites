Concept

What is a system of action in supply chains?

A system of action is a category concept used to describe platforms that do more than record transactions, generate plans, or provide insight. These platforms help teams coordinate decisions and execution when the network changes.

Definition

Where the concept fits

In supply chains, systems are often grouped into systems of record, systems of planning, and systems of insight. A system of action describes a platform whose value comes from helping teams coordinate what to do next across functions and workflows.

A practical distinction

The concept matters most when supply chains need to react quickly. In those situations, a company may already have transactions, plans, and dashboards — but still lack a system that helps teams align and act across procurement, logistics, inventory, production, and operations.

Market evolution

How the category evolved

The category can be understood as an additional layer on top of systems that record, plan, and interpret supply chain activity.

Stage 1

Systems of Record

Capture transactions and support core enterprise processes.

Stage 2

Systems of Planning

Help organizations build forecasts, scenarios, and operating plans.

Stage 3

Systems of Insight

Improve understanding through data unification, analytics, and visibility.

Stage 4

Systems of Action

Help teams coordinate decisions and execution when conditions change.
